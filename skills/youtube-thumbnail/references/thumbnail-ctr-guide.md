## CTR Lifecycle
- Highest CTR in first 24 hours (warm/subscribed audience)
- Sustained 4-8% CTR across expanding audience = healthy
- CTR naturally drops as video reaches colder audiences

## Face Thumbnails
- Faces = +20-30% CTR (vidIQ)
- Face thumbnails average 921,000 more views per video (AmpiFire)
- Strong emotion required — neutral expressions underperform
- Exaggerated but authentic expressions work best

## The 3-Step Viewer Psychology Flow

1. **Visual Stun Gun** — Something stops the scroll (1-2 seconds)
2. **Title Value Hunt** — Viewer reads the title for promise/benefit
3. **Visual Validation** — Viewer returns to thumbnail to confirm

Flow is: Thumbnail → Title → Thumbnail. If thumbnail doesn't pop → title never gets read.

## 7 Visual Stun Gun Elements (max 3 per thumbnail)

1. **Color contrast** — Vivid colors that pop against background
2. **Large face with emotion** — Recognition OR strong clear emotion
3. **Visually compelling graphic** — Bright colors, interesting design
4. **Big text, numbers, or dollars** — Large round numbers in huge font
5. **Red circles or arrows** — Direct attention (use sparingly)
6. **Aesthetic imagery** — Cinematic, symmetrical, soothing
7. **Design collage** — Words, numbers, icons surrounding subject

## 3 Composition Types

- **Symmetrical** — Subject centered, balanced sides
- **Asymmetrical (Rule of Thirds)** — Subject offset ~1/3, remaining space filled
- **A→B Split** — Screen split showing transformation/contrast

## Desire Loop Framework

Before designing, define:
1. **Core desire** — Making money, saving time, growing faster
2. **Specific pain point** — Growing too slow, can't code, manual work
3. **Solution/transformation** — Method, tool, framework that solves pain
4. **Curiosity loop** — "If I click, will I be able to ___?"

## Visual Design Rules
- Text overlay: 3 words max (ideally 2)
- Color palette: 2-3 primary colors for brand consistency
- Viewer decision time: Less than 1 second
- Custom thumbnail usage: 90% of top-performing videos
- Negative space: 30-40% of thumbnail area
- One clear focal point

## Thumbnail Specifications
- Minimum: 1280x720, Recommended: 1920x1080
- Aspect ratio: 16:9
- Formats: JPG, PNG
- Max file size: 2MB

## Information Split Rule (CRITICAL)
- Thumbnail = visual and emotional hook
- Title = keyword + promise
- NEVER duplicate text between thumbnail and title
- Thumbnail text must COMPLEMENT the title
- Title communicates the WHAT, thumbnail communicates the FEELING

## A/B Testing (YouTube Studio, mid-2024+)
- Up to 3 thumbnail variants
- Optimizes for watch time share (not just CTR)
- Test duration: up to 2 weeks
- 0.5% CTR difference is significant at scale

## Mobile Optimization
- 70%+ of views from mobile
- Design at 1920x1080, test at 320x180 (mobile feed size)
- Front-load title hook in first 45 characters

## Graphic Element Selection

Represent the desire loop in one of four ways:
1. **End state** — Show what they want (earnings screenshot, plaque)
2. **Process visualization** — Show the method they'll learn
3. **Before → After** — Show the transformation
4. **Anti-state / Pain point** — Remind them of the pain

## MrBeast Thumbnail Principles
- Titles under 50 characters
- Spark intense curiosity
- Auto-play adaptation (first 5 seconds extend thumbnail)
- Outlier-driven A/B testing

## Suppression Triggers (YouTube Policy)
- Nudity, violence, misleading content, hate speech
- 2024-2025: AI-enforced clickbait suppression via Gemini
- Pornographic thumbnails = immediate channel termination

## Bottom-Right Dead Zone
YouTube's timestamp overlay covers ~120x30px in the bottom-right corner.
NEVER place important elements there.
